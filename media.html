City of Camden to Honor Emilio Roman for Service to Veterans

Camden, NJ – July 8, 2016 – Emilio Roman, a disabled Marine Corps veteran of the Gulf War Era, will receive an award from the City of Camden on July 12 in recognition of his service to veterans. Roman, a popular speaker and author who is devoted to raising public consciousness about veterans’ issues such as PTSD, is the founder of the “Veteran Ambassadors” movement. The Camden City Counsel is honoring Roman for this work. The award also acknowledges Roman’s new book, which educates the public on how little acts of kindness helps veterans feel better about themselves and can improve their personal and professional lives.

“It is a great honor to be recognized for my work,” said Roman. “I have long sought to be a voice for our nations disabled and homeless veteran population. It means a lot to every vet in Camden that the city takes this issue seriously.”

Roman’s mission is to educate, communicate and advocate with government agencies, faith-based organizations and citizen’s groups about veteran issues. “I wholeheartedly believe our veterans are our country’s most valuable resource,” Roman added. “The US must do all it can to preserve the welfare of all American Veterans.”

Roman serves as a ‘Veteran Ambassador’ when his health permits. Prior to his service connected disability status, he was living his dreams of helping individual’s achieve physical fitness. His honors and achievements include: Fitness Director for City of Philadelphia under Former Mayor John F. Street; Degree in Health & Exercise Science; Celebrity Fitness Expert; Host of Fitness Success Radio; Fitness Model for such products as Body Bridge and Back in Line; 3rd Place winner of the Far Eastern Bodybuilding Classic Okinawa Japan; Media Appearances on such networks as CNN and Sirius Radio and Contributing Writer for Philly Fit Magazine.
