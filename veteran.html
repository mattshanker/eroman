Veteran

Emilio Roman is a United States Marine (disabled Gulf War era veteran). Emilio today serves as a Veteran Ambassador when his health permits. Emilio is known in his community as a champion for veteran’s rights.

Veteran Ambassador Movement (VAM)

"One act of kindness…One veteran at a time"

What is a Veteran Ambassador?

A person who has taken the Veteran Ambassadors Pledge to help our Veterans. The Veteran Ambassadors pledge to help our Veterans whenever the opportunity best allows, and to do so in a manner that is both respectful of the veteran, personal resources, and the greater good of our Country’s communities.

What is VAM?

The Veteran Ambassadors Movement was created by United States Marine Emilio Roman, a disabled Gulf War Veteran. VAM was borne out of Emilio’s personal challenges and struggles while attempting to get help for his earned military benefits. VAM recruits volunteers throughout the Nation and abroad to help carry out VAM’s Mission of helping our American Veterans with little acts of kindness.

What is VAM’s Mission

Educate our citizens about Veteran issues such as homelessness, post-traumatic stress, substance abuse, unemployment, continuing education, and more. This public awareness initiative aims to ultimately improve the quality of life for every American Veteran in need of personal, professional, or community support. VAM achieves its mission by working with faith-based community groups, local government agencies, and companies that support our Veterans.

How can I help?

One of the first things to do to get involved is to download the FREE book 101 Ways To Thank A Veteran: A How-To Guide For Honoring Our Country’s Most Valuable Resource; Our Veterans. This will offer you insights into the authors motivation and will also offer insights into the direction of VAM.

Where do you meet-up?

Churches, VFW’s, Coffee shops, schools, etc. Anywhere where the environment is safe and has is structure for people to speak open and honestly about veteran issues.VAM meet-ups are sprouting-up everywhere throughout the USA.

VAM’s Pledge to our Veterans

I (insert name here) pledge to help our Nation’s Veterans whenever the opportunity best allows for my service. I will do so in a manner that is both respectful of the veteran, my personal resources, and ultimately for the greater good of our Country’s communities.
